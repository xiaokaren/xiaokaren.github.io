About Me

I'm an incoming CS PhD student at the University of California San Diego in the CER Lab, advised by Leo Porter, Gerald Soosairaj, and Bill Griswold. My research interests lie broadly at the intersection of data science, education, and human-AI interaction.

Most recently, I graduated summa cum laude from Wellesley College with a Bachelor of Arts in Computer Science and Mathematics. At Wellesley, I received departmental honors in Computer Science for my thesis titled "Quantifying Diversity of Educational Evaluation Using Relative Student Performance" as well as the Excellence in Teaching Award in Computer Science.

Previously I've conducted internships in both academia and industry at the CMU Human-Computer Interaction Institute, as an NSF REU Intern, and Quantbot Technologies, as a Data Analyst Intern.

Outside of academics, I love finding good matcha soft serve, spending time at the beach, going on long drives with friends, and cooking (though I'm still pretty new and not very good).
