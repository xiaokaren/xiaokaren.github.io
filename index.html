About Me

I'm a senior at Wellesley College double majoring in Computer Science and Mathematics. My research interests lie at the intersection of data science, educational technologies, human-AI interaction, and algorithm design. Practically, my work analyzes the effectiveness of algorithms and systems using data science techniques. I'm eager to join interdisciplinary projects which would allow me to collaborate across diverse fields, apply data-driven insights, and create innovative solutions that bridge technology and human-centered design.

Outside of academics, I'm co-captain of the Wellesley Club Squash team, Networking Chair of the Wellesley CS Club, and Social Media Manager of the Wellesley CS Department. In my free time, I love crocheting, traveling, listening to music, and cooking (though I'm still pretty new and not very good).

I'm always happy to chat about potential collaborations, past work, my research (or personal!) backgrounds and interests, or even just a quick hello, so please feel free to reach out :)

Experience

I am an undergraduate research intern at Carnegie Mellon University's Human-Computer Interaction Institute through the NSF REU Program. At CMU HCII I am working in Profesor Vincent Aleven's Creating Adaptive Tutoring Systems (CATS) Lab under the mentorship of PhD student Conrad Borchers to study the effectiveness of inteligent tutoring systems through data-driven methods.

The Institute for Mathematics and Democracy (IMD) at Wellesley College aims to conduct research at the intersection of mathematics and democracy by employing cutting edge methods to tackle current political and societal issues. At the IMD, I am currently working as a research assistant for a project on empirical comparison of various ranked choice voting methods. In this position, I perform original analysis of the performance of RCV methods using data science techniques.

From Spring 2024 to present, I have been a tutor and grader for CS 231 Design and Analysis of Algorithms. In this role, I hold weekly office hours, conduct 1-on-1 tutoring sessions, and grade problem sets.

In my third year at Wellesley College I worked as an undergraduate student researcher in Professor Christine Bassem's CrowdNets Lab. The CrowdNets lab focuses on developing mechanisms and solutions to help guide the mobility of crowds and vehicles that participate in crowd-based platforms. My research with the CrowdNets Lab lies at the intersection of spatial data, algorithm design, graph theory, and social computing. This year, we defined a new metric of centrality that can help reposition idle drivers within gig-based platforms (like Uber and Doordash) in order to maximize profits and reduce emissions. My key contributions included:

  • Using graph theory to define a new metric of node centrality that improves on gaps in existing research

  • Analyzing existing research papers in the field to identify important research gaps and devise solutions

  • Designing and testing algorithms to improve task allocation in the context of gig-based platforms

Quantbot Technologies LP is a global quantitative investment advisor specializing in complex quantitative investment strategies. I had the privilege of interning with Quantbot's Data Trading Lab (DTL) for two summers. At Quantbot, I cleaned and processed large-scale financial datasets using tools like Pandas, DuckDB, and SQL to prepare them for subsequent research and analysis. In this role, I:

  • Optimized data capture and validation process using DuckDB, Pandas, and Apache Iceberg

  • Assessed, enriched, and normalized company outlook and projection data covering all global markets

  • Researched new database query tools that the company can implement to optimize data capture toolkits

  • Collaborated with quantitative researchers, traders, and external data vendors
